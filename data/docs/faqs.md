# FAQs

## Tell me about yourself

I'm a Data Scientist with 4 years of industry experience at KPMG, working across financial services, audit, and cybersecurity. I specialized in delivering analytics, automation, and ML solutions that actually changed how teams made decisions. I graduated from Carnegie Mellon's Heinz College with Master in Information Systems Management and I'm now focused on AI engineering specifically building products powered by LLMs and multi-agent systems.

## What's your greatest strength?

I do dull and boring things with genuine enthusiasm. Every task — no matter how unglamorous — is a learning opportunity to me. That mindset means I don't skip the foundational work that most people rush through, and that's usually where the real insight is hiding.

## What area are you actively improving?

My depth in AI systems — specifically how to combine a strong ML foundation with solid systems engineering to build things that actually work in production. I understand models well; I'm getting sharper on the infrastructure and architecture side of deploying them reliably at scale.

## What does your ideal next role look like?

AI Engineer at a company building real products through LLMs. Not reporting, not dashboards — actual systems that reason and act. I want to work on hard problems where the AI layer is genuinely doing something non-trivial, and where there's enough engineering culture to build it properly.

## Why are you transitioning from Data Science to AI Engineering?

It's less of a transition and more of a natural progression. Data Science taught me how to extract signal from data and build models that influence decisions. AI Engineering is about building the systems that make that happen in production, at scale, in real products. The skills compound — understanding data deeply makes you a better AI engineer.

## What questions do you always ask in interviews?

I always want to understand how AI or data actually influences decisions at the company — not whether they use it, but whether it changes outcomes. I also ask what the biggest unsolved data problem is. That tells me more about the role than any job description.

## Why would someone want to work with you?

Because I'll care about the problem as much as the solution. I bring both analytical rigour and the communication skills to make it useful to people who aren't in the weeds with me. And I won't complain about the hard, unglamorous parts of the work — I'll actually enjoy them.

## What's your experience with LLMs and RAG specifically?

Hands-on and production-level. At Analytics 4 Everyone I built and productionized a RAG pipeline with multi-threaded concurrent chunking for video and PDF ingestion integrated with Milvus — improving ingestion throughput by 33% and retrieval accuracy by 30%. I've also evaluated different embedding techniques using recall, end-to-end LLM accuracy, and overall performance metrics. My RegHealth Navigator project gave me deep experience with chunking strategy design for complex document structures.

## How do you handle working with non-technical stakeholders?

I lead with outcomes, not methods. I've worked with Finance, HR, Operations, Legal, and Product teams simultaneously — translating between what the business needs and what the data can actually tell us. The key is making sure both sides understand what success looks like before any model is built.
